What is a Keyword Density Checker?

A keyword density checker is a free SEO tool that analyzes how frequently specific words and phrases appear within a piece of content, expressed as a percentage of the total word count. For example, if your article has 1,000 words and your target keyword appears 15 times, the keyword density is 1.5%. This metric helps you understand whether your content is properly optimized for search engines without crossing into keyword stuffing territory. According to Google's SEO starter guide, creating useful, user-focused content is the best way to avoid such penalties.

Modern keyword density tools go beyond simple single-word counting. They analyze n-gram phrases (two-word, three-word, and four-word combinations) to give you a complete picture of how your content uses language. This is important because search engines now understand topics through phrase patterns, not just individual keywords. A good keyword density analyzer helps you write content that is both search-engine-friendly and natural for human readers. For a broader site-level check, you can also run a full SEO audit to catch other on-page issues.

How Does the Keyword Density Checker Work?

This tool processes your text entirely in your browser using a multi-step analysis pipeline:

  1. Text Normalization: The tool first converts your text based on your settings — lowercasing if case-insensitive mode is on, stripping HTML tags, and normalizing whitespace.
  2. Stop Word Filtering: If enabled, common stop words like "the," "is," "at," "which," and "on" are removed from the analysis. This ensures the results focus on meaningful content words rather than filler language.
  3. Token Extraction: The remaining text is split into tokens. For single words, this is straightforward. For n-grams (phrases), the tool creates sliding windows of 2, 3, or 4 consecutive words.
  4. Frequency Counting: Each unique token is counted across the entire text, recording how many times it appears.
  5. Density Calculation: For each keyword or phrase, density is calculated as (count / total words) × 100%. This gives you the percentage that represents how much of your content is dedicated to each term.
  6. Sorting and Display: Results are sorted by frequency and displayed in a table with visual bar indicators for quick scanning.

All processing happens locally in your browser. No text is sent to any server, making this tool completely private and instant. If you need to clean up raw text beforehand, consider using our text formatting tool to normalize spacing and encoding first.

Most SEO experts recommend a primary keyword density between 1% and 2%. Going above 3% risks being flagged as keyword stuffing. For secondary and LSI keywords, 0.5% to 1% is typically sufficient. The key is natural-sounding content that serves readers first. As noted by Moz's guide on keyword density, modern search engines use far more signals than just raw keyword frequency, but density remains a useful baseline metric.

Stop words are extremely common words like "the," "is," "at," "which," "on," and "a" that search engines typically ignore when analyzing content. Filtering them out gives you a much clearer picture of your meaningful keyword density. Without filtering, your top keywords would all be stop words, which is not useful for SEO analysis.

Keyword density helps search engines understand what your page is about. If your target keyword appears too rarely, search engines may not associate your page with that topic. If it appears too frequently, it triggers spam filters. The right density signals relevance without appearing manipulative, helping your page rank for the right queries.
