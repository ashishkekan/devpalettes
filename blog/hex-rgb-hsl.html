HEX vs RGB vs HSL Explained

Demystifying the three main color formats used in CSS.

When writing CSS, you have several options for defining colors. The three most common are HEX, RGB, and HSL. While they all result in the same screen color, they offer different advantages for developers and designers.

HEX (Hexadecimal)

HEX codes are six-digit combinations of numbers and letters (e.g., #FF5733) representing the Red, Green, and Blue components. They are compact and widely used in design software like Photoshop. They are the standard for copying colors from design tools to code.

RGB (Red, Green, Blue)

RGB defines colors by their Red, Green, and Blue intensity values (0-255). The main advantage of RGB is the ability to add an Alpha channel (RGBA) for transparency. This makes it superior for overlay effects where opacity is needed.

HSL (Hue, Saturation, Lightness)

HSL is often the most intuitive for developers. Hue is the color (0-360 degrees), Saturation is the intensity (0-100%), and Lightness is the brightness (0-100%). HSL makes it easy to create variations of a color—e.g., to create a lighter version, you just increase the lightness percentage. This is ideal for dynamic CSS systems.

Which Should You Use?

Use HEX for static colors in your stylesheets. Use RGBA when you need transparency. Use HSL when building theme systems or generating color scales programmatically. Our converter tool makes switching between formats instant.
