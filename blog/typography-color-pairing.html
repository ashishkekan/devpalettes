Typography and Color Pairing

How to combine text and color for maximum readability.

Typography and color are inseparable partners in UI design. The color of your text against its background determines not just readability but also the overall tone of your content. A poor pairing can make even the most beautiful font unreadable.

Contrast is King

High contrast between text and background is essential for legibility. Dark text on a light background or light text on a dark background are the safest choices. Avoid colored text on colored backgrounds unless the contrast ratio is verified—this is especially important for small body text.

Using Color for Hierarchy

Color can differentiate headings from body copy. While weight and size are primary tools, using a brand color for headings (e.g., dark blue H1 vs dark gray body text) adds visual interest and reinforces brand identity without sacrificing readability.

Colored Backgrounds

When placing text over colored backgrounds or images, always use an overlay. A semi-transparent black or white overlay ensures the text stands out. CSS gradients are excellent for creating smooth text legibility overlays on hero images.

Conclusion

Never view typography in isolation from color. Test your pairings across different screens and lighting conditions. A design that looks good on a calibrated monitor might fail on a low-brightness phone screen in sunlight.
