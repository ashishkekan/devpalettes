Email Marketing

Email Design Colors

Designing for the inbox, not the browser.

Email is one of the most restrictive mediums for color design. Unlike web browsers, which render code consistently, email clients (Outlook, Gmail, Apple Mail) interpret HTML and CSS differently. What looks stunning in a browser might break or turn invisible in an inbox. Mastering email color requires a blend of technical knowledge and aesthetic restraint.

The Dark Mode Challenge

Many users read emails in dark mode. If your email uses a white background with dark text, some email clients will invert it—turning white to black and black to white. This can ruin logos and brand colors. To counter this, avoid using image-based text. Use actual HTML text with hex colors. Test your emails in both light and dark modes to ensure contrast holds up.

Color and Deliverability

Spam filters analyze email content. While color itself isn't a direct trigger, emails that are one giant, red image can trigger "image-heavy" spam filters. Using a balance of HTML text (colored with hex codes) and images improves deliverability. Also, avoid using "invisible text" (white text on a white background), a classic spammer tactic that guarantees your email lands in the spam folder.

Conclusion

Email design is about resilience. Use a "safe" palette, test across clients, and prioritize text over images for critical information.
