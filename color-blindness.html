Understanding Color Blindness

Design for everyone with accessible color choices

Protanopia

Missing L-cones. Reds appear dark or blackish. About 1% of males.

Deuteranopia

Missing M-cones. Most common type. ~6% of males. Greens appear brownish tan.

Tritanopia

Missing S-cones. Blues appear greenish. Extremely rare condition.

Achromatopsia

No color cones. Complete grayscale vision. Affects 1 in 30,000.

Accessibility Tips

Use patterns or textures in addition to colors for data visualization

Ensure 4.5:1 contrast ratio for normal text (WCAG AA)

Avoid red-green combinations as primary indicators

Add text labels alongside color-coded elements
