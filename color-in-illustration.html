Color in Illustration

Integrating artwork into your digital color system.

Illustrations are powerful storytelling tools. They add personality and warmth to digital products. However, an illustration that ignores the UI's color palette looks like a sticker slapped on a page. To create a cohesive experience, the colors in your illustrations must speak the same language as your interface.

Deriving Palettes for Art

When commissioning or creating illustrations, start with your brand palette. Use the brand's primary and secondary colors as the dominant hues in the artwork. Introduce "accent" colors specifically for the illustration to add variety, but keep them harmonious. This ensures the illustration feels like part of the product, not a separate entity.

Lighting and Mood

Color in illustration dictates mood. A blue-dominant illustration feels calm or cold. A yellow-dominant one feels happy. Match the mood of the illustration to the intent of the page. An error page illustration might use muted, darker tones; a celebration screen might use vibrant, saturated ones.

Conclusion

Illustration is an extension of your UI. By tightly integrating color choices, you create a seamless visual journey for the user.
