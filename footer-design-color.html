Footer Design & Color

The unsung hero of user navigation and trust.

The footer is often the last thing a user sees, but it plays a crucial role in SEO, accessibility, and brand trust. It acts as a safety net, catching users who scroll to the bottom looking for specific information. A poorly colored footer can feel like a dead end, while a well-designed one feels like a solid foundation.

Contrast and Readability

Footers typically contain dense information: links, legal text, and contact info. Because this text is smaller, contrast is critical. Dark gray text on a light gray footer background is a common pitfall that fails accessibility tests. Ensure your footer text has a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1.

Background Color Strategy

Footers often use a darker background than the main content to signal "end of page." A dark blue, charcoal, or black footer grounds the design. It provides a visual stop. In dark mode, a footer might simply be the deepest black (#000000) to contrast against the slightly lighter body background (#121212).

Conclusion

Don't treat the footer as an afterthought. It is a vital part of the user journey. Use color to organize its content and ensure it remains accessible and readable.
