What is a Slug?

A slug is the part of a URL that identifies a particular page on a website in an easy-to-read form. Slugs are an important part of SEO because they help search engines understand the content of the page.

What is a URL Slug?

A URL slug is the specific, human-readable portion of a web address that identifies a particular page on a website. It typically appears at the end of the URL, following the domain name and any category or parent directory paths. For instance, in the URL example.com/blog/seo-best-practices, the slug is seo-best-practices.

Unlike database IDs or randomly generated strings, a well-crafted slug provides both users and search engines with an immediate, clear hint about the content of the page before they even click on it. Slugs are derived from the title of the page but undergo a cleaning process to ensure they only contain safe, web-compatible characters.


Why Slugs Matter for SEO

URL slugs are a confirmed, albeit minor, ranking factor in Google's search algorithm. When a user searches for a query, Google bolds the matching words in the search results—including the words in the URL. If your slug contains the target keyword, it reinforces the page's relevance, potentially improving its rank and significantly boosting its click-through rate (CTR).

Beyond direct ranking impact, slugs enhance readability. Users scanning search engine results pages (SERPs) often look at the URL to gauge the credibility and relevance of a link. A clean, descriptive slug like /best-running-shoes is far more trustworthy and clickable than a cryptic parameter-based URL like /post?id=84729. Furthermore, clean URLs are easier to share, copy, paste, and remember, indirectly supporting off-page SEO and social media marketing efforts.

Strategic keyword usage within the slug helps search engines categorize the page accurately. By placing the primary keyword near the beginning of the URL, you signal the core topic of the content efficiently. However, keyword stuffing in URLs is penalized, so the slug must remain natural and concise.


Best Practices for SEO-Friendly URLs

Use Hyphens, Not Underscores

Google's official documentation explicitly recommends using hyphens (-) to separate words in URLs. While humans read both easily, search engine crawlers historically treated underscores as joining words together. Therefore, seo-tips is read as two words, whereas seo_tips may be read as a single word, harming your keyword targeting.


Avoid Stop Words

Common stop words like "a," "an," "the," "and," "but," and "or" add unnecessary length to your URL without providing SEO value. Removing them makes the slug more concise and ensures the focus remains on your primary keywords. For example, "the-best-seo-tips-for-beginners" becomes "best-seo-tips-beginners."


Keep it Short and Descriptive

Long URLs get truncated in mobile search results and can appear spammy. Aim for a slug length of 3 to 5 words, keeping the total URL under 60 characters when possible. The slug should summarize the page's content accurately without being a duplicate of the title tag.


Always Use Lowercase Letters

URLs are case-sensitive on some servers (like Linux/Apache), meaning SEO-Tips and seo-tips could technically lead to different pages or cause duplicate content issues. Converting slugs entirely to lowercase prevents canonical problems and creates a cleaner visual appearance.

Examples of Good vs Bad Slugs

Understanding the difference between effective and poor URL structures is crucial. A bad slug might look like /post?id=492&cat=5 (contains no keywords, uses parameters) or /My_Awesome_Blog_Post!!! (uses underscores, uppercase letters, and special characters). A good slug strips the noise and focuses on meaning: /awesome-blog-post. Another example is converting a long title like "How to Start Learning CSS in 2026: A Complete Guide for Beginners" into a concise slug: /start-learning-css-2026-guide. It captures the primary keywords, drops stop words, and remains highly readable.


Frequently Asked Questions

What is a slug in a URL?

A slug is the part of a URL that comes at the very end and identifies a specific page in a human-readable format. For example, in 'example.com/blog/seo-best-practices', the slug is 'seo-best-practices'.

How to create SEO-friendly URLs?

To create SEO-friendly URLs, use lowercase letters, separate words with hyphens, remove stop words like 'a' or 'the', keep the slug under 60 characters, and include the primary target keyword.

Should I use hyphens or underscores in URLs?

You should always use hyphens (-) instead of underscores (_) in URLs. Google treats hyphens as word separators but does not treat underscores the same way, meaning 'seo-tips' is read as two words, while 'seo_tips' may be read as one single word.

Does URL slug affect SEO?

Yes, URL slugs directly affect SEO. Search engines use the words in the URL as a minor ranking factor. A descriptive slug helps Google understand the page's topic, improves click-through rates from search results, and enhances user experience.
